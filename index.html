History

Chocolate with the story

Godiva, founded in 1926 in Brussels, Belgium, by the Draps family, opened its first shop in the Grand-Place in honor of Lady Godiva. It expanded globally, with its first shop outside Belgium in Paris in 1958 and reaching the United States in 1966. In 1972, the first North American Godiva shop opened in New York City. In 2007, Godiva had annual sales of $500 million before being sold to Yıldız Holding in 2008 for $850 million. The company now operates over 450 shops worldwide and opened its 100th store in China in 2016. Notable milestones include the opening of Café Godiva in London's Harrods in 2012 and its first café in Brussels in 2018. In 2019, MBK Partners acquired Godiva's operations in several countries. As of October 2020, the CEO is Nurtaç Ziyal Afridi.

About

Since 1926, Godiva has crafted luxurious Belgian chocolates, embodying a tradition of excellence and passion for exquisite flavors. From truffles to bars, Godiva chocolates are a celebration of life's moments, inviting you to savor every decadent bite.

Nutrition Information

Godiva chocolate bars come in a 40-gram serving size with varying calorie counts: dark chocolate at 200 calories, milk chocolate at 220 calories, and white chocolate at 230 calories per serving. Fat content ranges from 12 to 14 grams, saturated fat from 7 to 8 grams, and all variants have 0 grams of trans fat. Cholesterol levels are 5 to 10 milligrams, sodium ranges from 10 to 20 milligrams, carbohydrates from 22 to 27 grams, sugars from 18 to 24 grams, and protein from 2 to 3 grams per serving. Ingredients include cocoa, sugar, milk solids, cocoa butter, emulsifiers, and flavorings, with potential allergens like milk, soy, nuts, and wheat. It's recommended that individuals with allergies check the packaging for specific allergen information before consuming.
